The MAISON Philosophy

Objects of
Enduring
Beauty

We believe that the most meaningful things are the ones that stay. A jacket worn for twenty years. A bag that bears the marks of a life well-lived. A scent that becomes part of how people remember you.

MAISON exists to curate these objects — things that were made with the intention of being kept forever.

Est. 2018 · Paris

The House
of MAISON

Who We Are

Born from a
Love of Objects

MAISON began in a small apartment in the 6th arrondissement of Paris in 2018. Our founder, Élise Moreau, had spent fifteen years as a textile editor at a major fashion house — quietly obsessed with the question of what makes certain things last and others fade.

She began collecting. A cashmere sweater from a mill in Hawick. A bag from a leather atelier in Florence that had been producing the same silhouette for four generations. Candles from a parfumeur in Grasse. Things that were clearly made differently — with time, with intention, with an understanding that the person receiving them deserved the best version of the maker's art.

MAISON is the curation of those discoveries — a small, considered edit of pieces for people who understand that true luxury is never loud.

What We Stand For

Our Values

✦

Radical Curation

We consider thousands of pieces to bring you dozens. Our edit is intentionally small — because we believe that having fewer, better things is the foundation of a considered life. Every product on MAISON has passed through the hands of our team and earned its place.

◯

Maker Relationships

We work directly with the ateliers, mills and artisans who make our pieces — often visiting in person to understand the craft behind each object. This relationship means better quality, fairer terms for makers, and pieces with stories worth telling.

⟳

Made to Last

We do not follow seasons. We do not produce trend pieces. Every item in our collection is designed to be worn or used for decades — the opposite of disposable. We measure success not in units sold, but in pieces that are still loved ten years from now.

Sustainability

Doing
Less, Better

The most sustainable thing we can do is make things that don't need to be replaced. Our entire business model is built on this belief — which is why we refuse to produce collections, resist seasonal drops, and never manufacture more than we need.

All our packaging is made from recycled and recyclable materials. We plant one tree for every order placed. And we maintain a repair programme — if a MAISON piece ever shows its age, we'll help restore it to its original condition at no charge.

We are not a perfect company. But we are a company trying, in good faith, to do better.
